3 skills I believe are becoming dramatically more important for marketers

1. Understanding buyers and markets deeply

Can you identify patterns?
Can you understand what buyers actually care about?
Can you position a company in a way that drives economic outcomes?

This skill is very undervalued in B2B marketing

A lot of marketers are rushing toward technical execution while skipping the more important work of understanding markets, psychology, positioning, and buyer behavior

2. Using data to tell compelling narratives

Data without interpretation is noise

And, it's not about manipulating data to support a preconceived story

It's being able to objectively assess reality, identify what matters, and communicate it in a way that drives better business decisions

This is one of the biggest reasons companies hire strategic partners

3. Knowing how to leverage AI without drowning in it

The AI landscape is moving fast but its not about using every new tool for the sake of it

It's identifying where AI creates actual leverage and using it for the right things

In the AI era, the winners will be the ones that chose not to outsource their strategy, taste and judgement to a machine

All business and marketing leaders have a choice to make

## Post 2

**Date:** 2026-02-24

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/meganwhitebowen_one-of-the-most-useful-things-a-company-can-activity-7429896504414220288-N-Oj

**Why selected:** Lays out the exact ICP-first content engine Refine Labs uses — build a target account list, find where buyers spend time, show up consistently with content that reflects their pain. The clearest articulation of demand creation for B2B SaaS.

One of the most useful things a company can do*

Build a target account list

An actual list of companies that should and could buy from you

Then ask three questions:
1. Who is the buyer or group of buyers inside those companies?
2. Where do they spend time online?
3. What is their context and what is their pain?

The marketing strategy

Create content → put it where those people are → earn awareness → generate inbound

This is essentially how we built the early Refine Labs engine and how we help our customers get more customers

A lot of companies jump straight to founder-led marketing as the answer - it can work be very cost efficient - but it's not a silver bullet anymore

LinkedIn is crowded, reach is lower, and the tactic only works if it's grounded in strategy

The real play is simpler than people want it to be:
- Know the customer, their pain and their context
- Know where they spend time
- Show up consistently in those places to communicate that you understand their context and can solve their pain

Whether the messenger is the founder, an employee, customers, or paid media depends on your constraints

Tactics can change, the fundamentals don't

*This is assuming you have clarified your position in the market, have a viable business model and have already acquired some paying customers and are ready to accelerate growth

## Post 3

**Date:** 2026-01-24

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/meganwhitebowen_marketing-challenges-vary-significantly-by-activity-7417919454384287744-QE6g

**Why selected:** Maps B2B SaaS marketing strategy to company stage — early stage needs validation and organic/founder-led content, later stage needs brand investment and demand creation. Essential context for any B2B SaaS content strategy decision.

Marketing challenges vary significantly by company stage

The stage you're at dictates the problems, the timelines, and the levers that actually drive growth

Here's how we see it based on our experience working with 300+ B2B SaaS companies over the last 6 years:

In early stage companies you need to: validate → acquire → learn

You're still answering first principle questions:
- Who's the buyer?
- What's the value?
- How much will they pay?
- Does the product deliver the outcome?

Because the fundamentals are still moving, the GTM system can't optimize, it can only learn

There are real tactical implications:
- Short planning cycles and time horizons to drive impact
- More qualitative signal than quantitative ROI
- Founder-led distribution and/or organic strategies are often required
- Paid advertising is risky until ICP + offer + price stabilize

In later stage companies, you need to: differentiate → scale efficiently → expand

Now the problems are:
- Not enough awareness
- Over-reliance on search + retargeting
- Customer acquisition costs start to rise
- Stalled pipeline creation
- Inconsistent messaging across GTM

Most later stage companies overspend on demand capture (Google, retargeting, "direct response") because it's attributable and under-fund demand creation + brand because it's harder to measure - these strategies also worked very well for a while for these companies so they are also not really scrutinizing them as they often should

In this case, the tactical implications are:
- Rebalance spend across Brand → Demand → Expand
- Fund awareness + education early in the cycle
- Build category + salience so you show up on Day 1 consideration sets
- Invest in creative + messaging, not just channels
- Measure pipeline + revenue, not just in-platform metrics
- Expand into customer marketing (NRR, expansion, advocacy)

At this stage, the bottleneck is market penetration + positioning + efficiency

If you want your marketing to work, align your strategy with your stage
