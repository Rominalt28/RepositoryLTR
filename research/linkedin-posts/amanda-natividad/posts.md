## Post 1

**Date:** 2026-06-24

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_someone-asked-me-why-i-post-my-marketing-activity-7475619181330321409-GmBk

**Why selected:** Explains her zero-click content philosophy — share value freely, prioritize impact over form fills — the core argument behind ungated B2B SaaS content strategy.

Someone asked me why I post my marketing insights and POV so freely. Why I don't do more to capture leads. Honestly... I didn't have a great answer for it.

I finally just said, "Because... I think more people should just care about doing marketing thoughtfully, and I care more about making that impact than getting the form fill."

Do I want more followers? Sure. More newsletter subscribers, more podcast listeners, sure sure. More SparkToro customers? Yes, please. 😂 And more book buyers? YES.

But really, I just believe in this:

- Getting and sustaining attention is super hard, and it's only getting harder.

- Big Tech scrapes all your content anyway, AI is only accelerating this, and it sucks for us but in the meantime we would be wise to optimize for impressions and ongoing engagement.

- It's becoming more and more important to know where your audience is actually hanging out online, what they care about, what they search for, and what topics they're interested in... so you can do marketing that actually resonates with them. In a way that is sustainable for your business and for your sanity.

Ultimately, I care more about getting people to believe and practice these things than I do about squeezing a conversion out of every idea I share, and tracing it back to perfect attribution.

So yes, please buy my book and buy a SparkToro account. But also... it's ok if you don't. Just do your best to do thoughtful marketing, make smart bets, be good to your customers, unclench your jaw, and go drink some water.

## Post 2

**Date:** 2026-06-03

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_if-youre-winning-deals-on-features-and-pricing-activity-7467594632097476608-95QL

**Why selected:** Makes the case that B2B is more emotional than B2C — your content needs to make buyers feel understood, not just informed. A foundational insight for B2B SaaS messaging and content strategy.

If you're winning deals on features and pricing, I have bad news.

You've already lost.

In most categories everyone has the same features at roughly the same price, and whatever edge you have gets copied by next quarter.

So once a buyer confirms you fit the budget and check the boxes, what makes them pick you over the other two contenders?

Not another spec.

It's whether your messaging makes them feel understood — whether you clearly know what they've tried before, why it let them down, and what's keeping them up at night.

That's the case Talia Wolf makes on this week's episode of Zero Click Marketing. She always describes a comparison page she rebuilt — making it longer, more detailed, more specific — that drove 54% more free trials.

Her bigger claim might be the one that sticks with you: B2B is more emotional than B2C.

Listen wherever you get your podcasts!

## Post 3

**Date:** 2026-05-24

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_question-how-do-you-decide-whether-to-gate-activity-7462513673765527553-BJw-

**Why selected:** Offers a clear decision framework for gating content — "ungate the argument, gate the utility" — directly actionable for B2B SaaS content teams deciding what to put behind a form.

Question: How do you decide whether to gate a piece of content?

Personally, I'm not a big fan of gated content, but I know this varies across industries. So my rule of thumb is this:

Ungate the argument. Gate the utility.

Ungate the framework, the POV, the headline findings, the idea you want the internet to remember.

Gate the template, deeper benchmark cuts, implementation kit, or the thing that helps someone actually do the work.

Think: what's actually valuable enough that someone would reasonably trade their email for it?

Curious what people think. And if you have receipts on your gated content success, you know I love tea. 👀
