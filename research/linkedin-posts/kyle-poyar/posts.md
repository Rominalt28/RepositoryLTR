## Post 1

**Date:** 2026-06-03

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kyle-poyar_the-initial-decision-about-which-customer-activity-7467928850438627330-guvB

**Why selected:** Data-backed framework on how early customer targeting decisions (mice, rabbits, deer, elephants) compound over time — directly informs B2B SaaS content strategy by showing which ICP unlocks the fastest growth.

The initial decision about which customer to target (and how much to charge them) can feel almost arbitrary when you're just trying to hit your first $10k MRR. It's not.

Back in 2014, Christoph Janz from Point Nine wrote what I'd consider to be a seminal framework. It maps the ways to build a $100M ARR SaaS company based on your target customer (which animal you're hunting). Four routes remain evergreen:

🐭 Hunting mice: Attract 1 million customers paying $100/year.
🐰 Hunting rabbits: Attract 100,000 customers paying $1,000/year.
🦌 Hunting deer: Attract 10,000 customers paying $10,000/yr.
🐘 Hunting elephants: Attract 1,000 customers paying $100,000/year.

So how do these different paths actually hold up in the data? I turned to ChartMogul & looked at 1,043 SaaS and AI companies that all reached at least $10k MRR.

The key insights from the data:

1. The most popular path to $10k MRR is hunting rabbits 🐰. That's 100 customers paying $100 per month.

The $100/mo. price point sits in a sweet spot: low enough for cash-strapped early adopters and SMBs, high enough that customers take the product seriously and give you real feedback.

2. Mouse 🐭 and rabbit 🐰 hunters face brutal churn in the early days.

The median annualized gross revenue retention (GRR) at $10k MRR is just 24.6% for mouse hunters and 31.6% for rabbit hunters. For deer hunters, it's 70.5%. For elephant hunters? 100%.

3. Your initial target customer sticks with you.

70% keep the same target customer 3+ years after their first $10k MRR. When startups do manage to change animals, they usually move one animal upmarket (🦌 is a popular destination).

4. Deer 🦌 hunters are growing 3-5x faster than everyone else.

Growth rates at $10k MRR were nearly identical across all four groups. 3 years later, the differences became stark.

---

The data shows that startups become what they hunt, and most never change.

Which animal are you hunting?

## Post 2

**Date:** 2026-06-03

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kyle-poyar_the-myth-of-overnight-success-activity-7466100998512693249-kM0B

**Why selected:** Busts the overnight success myth with data from 6,500+ B2B startups — only 0.1% hit $10M ARR in 12 months. Content strategy built on endurance and retention compounds far better than chasing viral growth.

Let's ditch the myth of overnight success. Data across 6,500+ B2B startups tells a different reality: reaching $10M ARR is common only if you survive long enough, and fast paths are genuinely rare.

👉 8.4% of startups reached $10M+ ARR within 10 years of first revenue
👉 Only 0.1% reached $10M+ ARR within 12 months of first revenue

The data shows 4 ways to improve your odds:

1️⃣ You are what you hunt.

Who you target at $10k MRR (mice, rabbits, deer, elephants) sticks with you even 3+ years later. Deer appear to be the perfect prey.

2️⃣ Growth is about endurance, not speed.

Growth rates almost always decay year-after-year: aim for 80%+ growth endurance to be top quartile. The best startups slow the decay by resetting GTM velocity & increasing the pace.

3️⃣ Retention matters more than acquisition.

It's nearly impossible to maintain fast growth with net revenue retention (NRR) below 80%. Sales & marketing have a bigger impact on churn prevention than customer success (retention is a team sport).

4️⃣ Conversion is your accelerator.

The median free-to-paid conversion rate is 8%. Even small improvements in conversion translate into much faster growth w/o extra costs.

**Why selected:** Practical breakdown of the top pricing challenges by model type across 230+ SaaS companies — essential context for B2B SaaS content strategy since pricing directly shapes ICP, messaging, and expansion narrative.

If you're not happy about your pricing, you're far from alone. Your pricing is probably broken, and here's what you can do about it.

The top B2B pricing challenges according to 230+ SaaS and AI companies:

FLAT-FEE SUBSCRIPTION

😬 Challenge: Not enough expansion revenue

👉 What to do: Introduce a premium edition, charge for add-ons, or add a fair use policy

SEAT-BASED PRICING

😬 Challenge: Not enough expansion revenue & not future proof with AI

👉 What to do: Shift to hybrid pricing with usage-based expansion (AI credits, outcomes)

USAGE- OR OUTCOME-BASED PRICING

😬 Challenge: Too hard to forecast/predict

👉 What to do: Give admins more visibility and control over usage/spend

HYBRID PRICING

😬 Challenge: Too difficult to explain

👉 What to do: Simplify the credit model to charge for fewer actions, create docs for LLMs to understand your pricing (and explain it to buyers)

—

The TL;DR: Don't abandon your broken pricing, fix it.
